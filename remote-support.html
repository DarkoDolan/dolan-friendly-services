Remote Support

Friendly, plain-English help through Microsoft Quick Assist. Available for Windows 10 and Windows 11 computers only.

What remote support is

Remote support lets me see your screen over the internet so I can help diagnose simple computer problems while you watch. If needed, I can ask for temporary control, but you choose whether to allow it.

The session uses Microsoft Quick Assist, not a permanent remote-access program.

What you need

Windows 10 or Windows 11 Internet connection Microsoft Quick Assist By appointment

Not available for Mac, iPhone, Android, Chromebook, or hardware-only repairs.

What I can help with

Good fit

  • Windows settings and basic troubleshooting
  • Email, browser, printing, and file-sharing help
  • Installing or updating basic apps
  • Simple device setup instructions
  • Photos, PDFs, documents, and plain-English steps
  • Game/controller/software setting checks

Not a good fit

  • Physical repairs or broken hardware
  • Cracked screens, liquid damage, or internal parts
  • Advanced business networks or servers
  • Password bypassing or account recovery I can’t verify
  • Guaranteed malware removal or data recovery
  • Legal, financial, medical, or official compliance advice

How it works

Contact first

Email a quick description of the problem. I’ll let you know whether it sounds like something I can reasonably help with.

Agree on the task and price

Remote tech help starts from $20 depending on the task. The price is agreed before work starts.

Open Quick Assist

You open Microsoft Quick Assist on your Windows 10/11 computer. I provide a short code for the session.

You allow the session

You enter the code, choose whether to share your screen, and decide whether to allow control if requested.

We fix or explain what we can

You can watch the whole time. I’ll keep things plain-English and explain what I’m doing where possible.

The session ends

When the job is finished, the Quick Assist session is closed. I do not keep ongoing access to your computer.

Safety note: Only start a remote support session when you have contacted Dolan Friendly Services yourself. Never allow remote access to someone who randomly calls, messages, or pressures you. Do not share banking passwords or private codes during a support session.
